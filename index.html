About Me

Hi! I’m Amy Nyaga — a software engineer, curious creative, and certified late-night coder who still feels a tiny spark of joy every time a new idea comes to life on screen.

My journey began unexpectedly when I decided to redesign my personal blog. What started as innocent curiosity turned into a deep passion for building things on the web. Soon enough, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript became more than just code — they became tools to turn my sketches and ideas into living, breathing experiences people can actually use.

I love working on projects that combine design and functionality: sleek landing pages, interactive dashboards, small but mighty web apps like calculators, note-taking tools, or budget trackers that are not just useful, but also feel delightful to use. I enjoy the challenge of making complex things look simple — and making everyday tools a little more beautiful.

Technically, I’m fluent in HTML, CSS, JavaScript and comfortable crafting responsive, cross-browser designs that adapt seamlessly to different devices. I often reach for React when building interactive apps, and I keep my work version-controlled and tidy with Git. I’m also fascinated by UI/UX design — how small details like spacing, color, or animations can transform user experience.

Outside coding, I’m probably sketching new layout ideas, reorganizing my color palettes (for the hundredth time), or explaining to friends why a button’s hover effect can make or break the vibe. I believe technology should feel human, design should feel intentional, and side projects should always have a touch of fun.

So whether you want to collaborate on a project, share design inspiration, or debate the best VS Code theme, let’s connect. Because in the end, good ideas get better when shared!
